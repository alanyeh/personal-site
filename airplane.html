Airplane Ticket Redesign

Case Study

Sketch, Photoshop

Let me start off with a typical boarding ticket...

This is a standard boarding ticket you may get at any airline. Overall, the ticket is hard to read, important information isn't visually available - information can get easily mixed up. And if you're in a hurry, you'll easily miss your flight.

Version 1

I focused on making the most important information legible. Then, I added a die-line that indicates a place to be torn/folded separating the information into two sections. However, the boarding information on the right seemed awkward and unbalanced. I was also missing the date of arrival, and the overall information is still scattered.

This is the final boarding pass. Left is pre-flight information from reaching the terminal to boarding. The right side is all the information after boarding. The center die-line is the foldable area, that can be torn off by the attendant. The color is black and white to make it easy to print on any printer.

Here's the finished design. One of the revisions, I had the barcode in the center. Which didn't make sense, if the die-line was torn, it could have tore the barcode off. It makes sense to put the barcode on the left side. If I was handing the ticket facing me with my right hand, then the barcode would be facing away from the flight attendant and towards the scanner.
